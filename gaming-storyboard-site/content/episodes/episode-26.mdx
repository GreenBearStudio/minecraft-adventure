## Still Running...

Hunjvo sprinted through the twisting corridors of the maze, tiny legs pumping, tiny arms flailing, tiny brain screaming. The music boomed. The countdown had ended. The race was on.

Other mini‑players zipped past him — some fast, some clumsy, some clearly pros, and one with a bright green mohawk who kept bumping into everyone like he owned the place.

Hunjvo tried to keep up.

He turned left.

He turned right.

He turned… into a hole.

## The Fall

Hunjvo didn’t even have time to scream.

He just dropped straight down into the void — or whatever this strange glowing pit was.

“This is it,” he thought.

“Noor… Efe… I will never see you again… tell my horses I love them…”

He closed his eyes dramatically.

And then—

## POP! Teleportation

Hunjvo landed on something soft... soft snow?

Not home.

Not the Fortress of Fluffy Socks.

Not even the Deep Dark.

He was back in front of The Host — the talking penguin.

The penguin clapped his flippers together.

“So! How was your run? Try. Try again. Win. Win next time!”

The penguin did a little dance.

A penguin dance.

And then —

A butty dance.

Hunjvo stared.

The narrator stared.

Even the maze walls seemed confused.

“Is… is this penguin a player too?” Hunjvo wondered.

“Oh no, little player,” the penguin chirped proudly. “I am The Host!”

Hunjvo was still confused, but he felt something spark inside him — determination.

He stood up.

He puffed out his tiny chest.

He was ready.

“That’s the spirit!” the penguin cheered. “Run! Get the loo—”

“Loot?” Hunjvo asked.

“No treasure!” the penguin corrected sharply.

The narrator blinked.

“But isn’t it loo—”

“NO. No treasure!” the penguin snapped, cutting off even the narrator.

It was clear now:

The Host ruled this maze.  

Not Hunjvo.

Not the narrator.

Not even the mohawk guy.

## Rivalry Begins

Hunjvo stepped back toward the maze entrance, ready for round two.

He remembered the mohawk guy — the one with the weird colored face who shoved him into the void earlier.

A rival.

A challenger.

A menace.

Hunjvo clenched his tiny fists.

He would run.

He would dodge.

He would survive.

He would beat that mohawk guy.

“RUN!” the penguin shouted.

And Hunjvo bolted into the maze again —

determined, focused, and absolutely ready to prove himself.

The maze awaited.

The Host watched.

The narrator held his breath.
