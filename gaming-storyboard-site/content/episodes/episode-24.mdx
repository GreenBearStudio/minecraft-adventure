## The Escape

Hunjvo ran.

He didn’t know where he was going — only that the darkness behind him was alive, shrieking, vibrating, hunting. His legs moved on pure panic, his arms flailed, and somewhere in the distance he heard Noor shouting something heroic and Efe… somewhere. Hopefully not eaten.

Hunjvo stumbled forward until he saw a familiar silhouette.

Noor.  

Calm.

Focused.

Pro‑like.

The kind of calm that said I have survived worse and I will survive this too.

“Hunjvo,” Noor said, voice steady, “follow me if you want to live.”

And Noor sprinted into the darkness.

Hunjvo immediately burst into tears and screamed like a baby.

From the shadows came Efe’s voice:

“Hunjvo, come on you noob! The Wardens are coming! Stop crying and follow Noor’s voice!”

## The Torch Trail of Hope

Noor placed torches as he ran — one after another, a glowing breadcrumb trail through the pitch‑black maze. Hunjvo saw the faint light and bolted toward it.

Behind him, something massive moved.

A thundering step.

A deep, rumbling growl.

A shriek that rattled his bones.

The Warden.

Or… Wardens?

Plural?

Hunjvo didn’t want to know.

He just ran.

## The Chase of Chaos

Hunjvo followed the torches, heart pounding.

He saw Noor ahead — leaping, flipping, sliding, doing impossible parkour moves that made Hunjvo question physics, gravity, and reality itself.

<AssetImage name="d284_DeepDark.png" />

“Noor the parkour master…” Hunjvo whispered in awe.

Then he tripped over his own feet.

But he got up.

He ran.

He followed the light.

And then —

“Efe!” Hunjvo cried.

She stood near the exit, waving frantically.

“HURRY!”

## The Celebration (…Sort Of)

“We made it!” Hunjvo shouted.

“Time to make a party for Noor! The greatest pro parkour master of all—”

“Stop, Hunjvo, you’re embarrassing me,” Noor muttered.

Hunjvo turned around…

<AssetImage name="Noor&Efe.png" />

And did the butty dance.

And then—

PFFFFFT.

“HUNJVO!” Noor yelled, gagging.

Efe covered her nose.

“It smells like rotten zombie flesh!”

Hunjvo blinked innocently.

“Oh… yeah. I ate some earlier.”

The narrator sighed.

Deeply.

Painfully.

But they were alive.

They had escaped the Deep Dark.

And the adventure…

Was far from over.
