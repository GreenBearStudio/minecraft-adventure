## Running in the darkness? Is this good.. uhmm..

Hunjvo ran.

He didn’t know where he was running, or why his legs were moving faster than his brain, but he ran. The darkness swallowed everything — the walls, the floor, even the sound of his own footsteps. Only the shrieking echoed, bouncing off the ancient stone like a warning from the past.

Somewhere ahead, he heard Noor’s voice.

Somewhere behind, he heard something else.

A roar?

A shriek?

A heartbeat that wasn’t his?

Hunjvo didn’t want to find out.

## Reuniting in the Dark

Through the pitch‑black maze, Hunjvo stumbled until he saw two silhouettes — Noor and Efe. Relief washed over him like a warm potion.

“Hunjvo!” Noor whispered. “Time to get more loot!”

And before Hunjvo could even blink, Noor bolted deeper into the darkness.

<AssetImage name="d283_DeepDark.png" />

Efe and Hunjvo exchanged a look — the kind of look that said we are absolutely going to die but Noor is Noor so we follow him anyway — and they sprinted after the legendary pro.

## The Creature of the Deep

Then the shriek came again.

Louder.

Closer.

Angrier.

The ground trembled. Dust fell from the ceiling. And then Hunjvo saw it.

A hulking, towering creature — black and blue, shaped like a nightmare.

No eyes.

A gaping mouth in the center of its chest.

Strange blue “ears” twitching, sensing every vibration.

A creature born of darkness, built of sound.

A Warden.

Hunjvo froze.

The Warden sniffed the air.

Oh no.

Oh no no no.

Hunjvo remembered — he had eaten rotten zombie flesh.

He reeked.

He smelled like a walking undead buffet.

The Warden turned.

It sniffed again.

It locked onto the stink.

Hunjvo’s face went pale.
